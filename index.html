The Life of Xenophon

Soldier, student of Socrates, historian, teacher of practical wisdom

Xenophon lived in the late fifth and fourth centuries BCE, moving through worlds of philosophy, war, exile, leadership, household management, and memory. Born into the world of Classical Athens, he became a companion of Socrates and later joined the expedition of Cyrus the Younger, marching with the Greek mercenaries whose retreat he narrates in the Anabasis.

His writings include the Anabasis, Memorabilia, Hellenica, Oeconomicus, Cyropaedia, and works on horsemanship, leadership, and Spartan society. Across them, Xenophon asks practical questions: how should a person act under pressure, how should a household be ordered, what makes a leader trustworthy, and how does discipline become character?

Studying Greek with Xenophon means learning more than forms. It means learning how a Greek sentence carries action, judgment, memory, command, friendship, and moral pressure across the page.
